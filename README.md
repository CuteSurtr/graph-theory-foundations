# Graph Theory Foundations: Theoretical Foundations & Future Algorithms  
**Jiho Lee, UC San Diego Math-CS ’27**

**A self-contained LaTeX e-book presenting rigorous graph-theoretic foundations, with a planned second volume on algorithm visualizations and interview preparation.**

---

## Overview

This manuscript unites:

- Formal definitions, theorems, and proofs  
- **200+** TikZ-rendered diagrams and schematic illustrations  
- In-depth treatment of core graph-theoretic topics  

It is intended for:

- Undergraduate and graduate courses in discrete mathematics and graph theory  
- Self-study by learners preparing for advanced coursework or technical interviews

## Table of Contents

### Part I — Theoretical Foundations

1. **Basic Classes of Graphs**  
   - Cycles, paths, degrees, neighborhood  
   - Common families: complete, cycle, path, star, wheel, bipartite, regular, hypercube, Petersen  

2. **Degree-Related Properties**  
   - Handshaking lemma  
   - Degree sequences  
   - Extremal degrees, pendants, and isolates  

3. **Subgraphs, Walks & Connectivity**  
   - Subgraph definitions  
   - Walks, trails, paths, and components  

4. **Matching & Network Flows**  
   - Matchings and Hall’s theorem  
   - Flow networks and max-flow/min-cut duality  

5. **Eulerian Graphs**  
   - Euler trails and tours  
   - Eulerian digraphs and De Bruijn sequences  

6. **Hamiltonian Graphs**  
   - Hamiltonian cycles and Dirac’s theorem  
   - Ore’s theorem, Chvátal’s theorem, and TSP overview  

7. **Long Cycles & Extremal Results**  
   - Conditions for long cycles  
   - Probabilistic and extremal method applications  

8. **Trees & Forests**  
   - Characterizations of trees  
   - Bridge properties, BFS & DFS trees  

9. **Structure of Connected Graphs**  
   - Blocks, cut-vertices, and decomposition
